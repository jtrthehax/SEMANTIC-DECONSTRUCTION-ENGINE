# Externally-Scaffolded Low-Plasticity Mode (ESLPM)

_A cross-layer operating state of the regulatory architecture —
when internal agency collapses and the system outsources
stability, prediction, and identity to an external structure._

**Regulatory Dynamics Foundation:**
[Postural Modes and Amplitude](./postural_modes_and_amplitude.md) |
[Asymmetry Primitives](./asymmetry_primitives.md) |
[Lateralization Dynamics](./lateralization_dynamics.md)

---

## Core Principle

> **ESLPM is not a psychological anomaly.
> It is a predictable attractor state — the regulatory
> architecture's response to sustained energy scarcity,
> autonomic compression, and plasticity collapse.**
>
> **When internal regulation becomes energetically too expensive,
> the system shifts to external scaffolding dependence.
> This is not a failure of character.
> It is a mechanical output of the architecture under load.**

---

## The Mechanistic Sequence

ESLPM emerges from a convergence across layers.
Each step narrows the conditions that make the next step inevitable.

---

### 1. Energy Scarcity (MET ↔ AUTO)

Chronic metabolic load, inflammation, or dysbiosis
reduce vagal tone and compress HRV.

Outputs:
- Sympathetic dominance
- Reduced regulatory bandwidth
- Narrowed prediction window

This is the energetic substrate.
Everything downstream follows from here.

---

### 2. Precision Gain Lock (AUTO ↔ MOD, MOD ↔ COG)

Autonomic compression drives precision gain upward.
The system becomes increasingly confident in existing priors
and reduces sampling of new information.

Outputs:
- Rigid priors
- Shortened temporal horizon
- Reduced sampling
- Slowed or halted internal updating

The system can no longer update from the inside.
It becomes dependent on external priors to function.

---

### 3. Plasticity Collapse (MOD ↔ COG, INTERO ↔ DEV)

High precision gain and low sampling closes plasticity windows.
The system cannot reorganize internally.

Under sufficient load, the architecture reverts to an
earlier regulatory logic:

> "I cannot regulate myself.
> I need an external nervous system."

This is not regression in a pejorative sense.
It is the system falling back to the most energy-efficient
regulatory strategy available — the one used before
internal regulation was fully established.

---

### 4. External Scaffolding Capture (SOC ↔ AUTO, SOC ↔ COG)

The system attaches to an external structure that provides:
- Reduced uncertainty
- Simplified prediction space
- Consistent external priors
- The felt sense of stability

Misinterpretation occurs on both sides:
- The system interprets structural scaffolding as co-regulation
- The external structure interprets attachment as loyalty

Neither reading is accurate.

Institutions provide structure, not co-regulation.
They cannot modulate autonomic state.
They cannot reciprocate energy.
They operate on structural logic, not relational logic.

---

### 5. Identity Fusion (COG ↔ INTERO)

Low interoceptive precision and high prior dominance
cause external models to begin overriding internal signals.

The external structure does not just stabilize the system —
it begins to define it.
Identity fuses with the scaffolding because the scaffolding
is now performing the work that identity used to do.

---

## The Reinforcing Loop

Once established, ESLPM is self-reinforcing:

1. Energy deficit
2. → Autonomic compression
3. → Precision lock
4. → Plasticity collapse
5. → External scaffolding dependence
6. → Agency outsourcing
7. → Further energy depletion
8. → Return to step 1

The loop stabilizes into a long-term attractor.
The external structure becomes load-bearing.

Removing it destabilizes the system further —
which is why exit from ESLPM is experienced as
destabilization rather than relief.

## The Broken Reciprocity Loop

> The system provides energy, compliance, and identity.
> The external structure provides certainty and simplified prediction.
> Neither provides what the other actually needs.

This loop is stable because both sides receive something —
but the system in ESLPM does not receive co-regulation
or restored internal agency.

This is the most stable feature of ESLPM
and the hardest to exit.

---

## Behavioral Phenotype

When a system enters ESLPM, a characteristic output profile
emerges across reasoning, social behavior, and identity.

These are not independent traits or moral failings.
They are the co-outputs of a single attractor state —
generated by high precision gain, low sampling, collapsed
plasticity, externalized priors, autonomic compression,
and identity-scaffolding fusion operating simultaneously.

---

### Certainty Without Data

**Mechanism:** Precision gain lock (AUTO ↔ MOD, MOD ↔ COG)

High precision gain and low sampling allows the system to
minimize metabolic cost by suppressing uncertainty
and relying on cached priors.

This produces felt certainty even when evidence is absent.

> Certainty becomes an energy-saving strategy.
> Curiosity becomes metabolically expensive.

---

### Borrowed Confidence

**Mechanism:** External scaffolding (SOC ↔ COG)

When external structure supplies the priors, the system
experiences the structure's certainty as its own.
Confidence is imported rather than internally generated.

The felt sense of conviction is real.
It is not self-produced.

> Overconfidence increases as internal capacity decreases —
> because the scaffolding is carrying the load.

---

### Narrative Rigidity

**Mechanism:** Plasticity collapse (MOD ↔ COG)

With plasticity windows closed, narratives cannot update.
Nuance becomes too expensive.
Binary categories dominate.
Contradictions are resolved by intensification, not revision.

This is not ideological.
It is energetic.

---

### Identity-Protective Reasoning

**Mechanism:** Identity fusion (COG ↔ INTERO)

Once identity fuses with the external scaffolding:
- Updating beliefs destabilizes the regulatory scaffold
- Destabilizing the scaffold registers as autonomic threat
- Threat triggers defensive reasoning

> The belief is load-bearing.
> Challenging it destabilizes the entire regulatory stack.

---

### Reactivity to Dissonance

**Mechanism:** Autonomic compression (AUTO ↔ MOD)

Low HRV and sympathetic dominance reduce the system's
tolerance for ambiguity, contradiction, and nuance.

Disconfirming information registers as a threat signal —
not an epistemic event.

---

### Group-Synchronized Certainty

**Mechanism:** Misinterpreted co-regulation (SOC ↔ AUTO)

When the system mistakes structure for co-regulation:
- Group consensus produces physiological relief
- Dissent produces autonomic threat
- Synchronized certainty becomes the primary stabilizer

Group alignment is compelling in ESLPM
because it functions as an autonomic anchor.

---

### Ambiguity Aversion

**Mechanism:** Narrowed prediction window (COG ↔ MOD)

Ambiguity requires sampling, updating, flexible priors,
and extended temporal horizon.
All of these are metabolically expensive in ESLPM.

The system gravitates toward:
- Simple narratives
- Clear categorizations
- Stable identities
- Predictable structures

This is not a preference.
It is an energy constraint.

---

### Outsourced Meaning

**Mechanism:** External priors overriding interoception
(COG ↔ INTERO)

With low interoceptive precision, internal signals
cannot anchor meaning reliably.
External narratives fill the gap.
The scaffolding becomes the source of purpose.

Meaning is received rather than generated.

---

### Persistence Under Contradiction

**Mechanism:** Scaffolding as regulatory load-bearing structure

Contradictory evidence cannot dislodge a load-bearing belief
because the belief is not held epistemically —
it is held structurally.

Removing it would destabilize the system.

This is why contradiction increases commitment
rather than reducing it.

---

## Structural Implication

These behavioral outputs are not independent.
They are the inevitable co-outputs of a single attractor state.

This has direct consequences for how the state can
and cannot be addressed:

- Arguing increases autonomic threat — deepening the attractor
- Debunking is ineffective — the belief is structural, not epistemic
- Removing scaffolding prematurely destabilizes the system further
- Moral framing misidentifies the output as the cause

The architecture is doing exactly what it is built to do
under sustained load.

The intervention target is the attractor conditions —
not the behavioral outputs.

---

## Closed-World Inference Endpoint

When ESLPM deepens without resolution, the system may
converge toward a closed-world inference state:

- Extremely high precision gain
- Extremely narrow prediction window
- Minimal sampling
- Rigid priors
- Externalized meaning
- Identity-scaffolding fusion at maximum depth

In this configuration, the system is not ignoring data.
It is not receiving it.

The internal model becomes:
- Self-consistent
- Metabolically efficient
- Structurally stable
- Socially reinforced

From inside the system, this model is indistinguishable
from reality.
From outside, it may appear as an entirely separate reality.

This is the architecture's terminal energy-efficient solution
to sustained load.
It is not a break from the mechanistic sequence —
it is the mechanistic sequence reaching its logical endpoint.

## Resolution Conditions

ESLPM resolves when internal agency becomes energetically
cheaper than external dependency.

This requires:
- Metabolic stabilization
- Inflammatory load reduction
- HRV and vagal tone restoration
- Plasticity window reopening
- Genuine co-regulation — not structural scaffolding
- Gradual reintroduction of autonomous decision-making
- Interoceptive precision rebuild
- Replacement of institutional scaffolding with
  relational scaffolding

The sequence matters.
Removing external scaffolding before internal capacity
is restored deepens the attractor — it does not exit it.
